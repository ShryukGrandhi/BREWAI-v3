# Food Safety Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
**Restaurant:** Burger Queen  
**Location:** New York City  
**Last Updated:** November 2, 2025  
**Compliance:** NYC Health Code Article 81

---

## 1. Food Handler Requirements

### 1.1 Certification
- **ALL** food handlers must complete NYC Food Protection Course
- Certificate valid for 5 years
- Must be renewed before expiration
- Certificate must be displayed or available on-site

### 1.2 Personal Hygiene
- Wash hands for minimum 20 seconds:
  - Before starting work
  - After handling raw food
  - After touching face, hair, or body
  - After using restroom
  - After handling trash
- Hair restraints required at all times
- No jewelry except plain wedding band
- Clean uniforms daily

---

## 2. Temperature Control (CRITICAL)

### 2.1 Cold Holding
- **Refrigeration:** 41°F (5°C) or below
- Check temperatures every 4 hours
- Discard if above 41°F for more than 2 hours

### 2.2 Hot Holding
- **Hot foods:** 135°F (57°C) or above
- Check temperatures every 2 hours
- Reheat to 165°F before hot holding

### 2.3 Cooking Temperatures (REQUIRED)
- **Ground meat (beef patties):** 155°F (68°C) for 15 seconds
- **Chicken (whole pieces):** 165°F (74°C) for 15 seconds
- **Fish:** 145°F (63°C) for 15 seconds
- **Eggs (for immediate service):** 145°F (63°C)

---

## 3. Thawing Procedures (HIGH RISK)

### 3.1 Approved Methods ONLY
1. **Refrigeration:** 41°F or below (SAFEST)
   - Time: 24-48 hours for large items
   - Preferred method for wings, chicken

2. **Cold running water:** 70°F or below
   - Submerge in leak-proof package
   - Change water every 30 minutes
   - Maximum time: 2-3 hours for chicken wings

3. **Microwave:** Only if cooking immediately after

4. **As part of cooking process:** Direct from frozen

### 3.2 PROHIBITED
- ❌ Counter thawing at room temperature
- ❌ Hot water thawing
- ❌ Thawing overnight without refrigeration

### 3.3 Wings Specific
- **Frozen wings:** Must thaw in refrigerator 24 hours before use
- **Emergency thawing:** Cold water method, maximum 2 hours
- **If thawing exceeds 3 hours at room temp:** DISCARD IMMEDIATELY (food safety violation)

---

## 4. Cross-Contamination Prevention

### 4.1 Separate Equipment
- **Red cutting boards:** Raw meat only
- **Yellow cutting boards:** Raw poultry
- **Green cutting boards:** Vegetables
- **White cutting boards:** Ready-to-eat foods

### 4.2 Storage Order (Top to Bottom in Refrigerator)
1. Ready-to-eat foods (top shelf)
2. Seafood
3. Whole cuts of beef/pork
4. Ground meat
5. Poultry (bottom shelf)

### 4.3 Utensil Sanitation
- Clean and sanitize between each use with raw proteins
- Sanitizer concentration: 50-100 ppm chlorine

---

## 5. Fryer Safety & Certification

### 5.1 Fryer Operation Requirements
- **Only certified staff** may operate deep fryers
- Certification course: 2-hour NYC Fire Department approved training
- Recertification: Every 2 years
- Certificate must be posted near fryer station

### 5.2 Fryer Station Staffing (CRITICAL)
- **Minimum staffing:** 1 certified cook per active fryer
- **Multiple fryers:** Additional certified cook for every 2 fryers
- **Peak hours (lunch/dinner):** Minimum 2 certified cooks if using 2+ fryers
- **Solo operation:** Only 1 fryer may be active with 1 certified cook

### 5.3 Why This Matters
- **Fire risk:** Unattended fryers are leading cause of restaurant fires
- **Oil temperature:** Must maintain 350-375°F (critical safety zone)
- **Emergency response:** Certified cooks trained to handle oil fires

---

## 6. Compliance Violations & Penalties

### 6.1 Critical Violations (Immediate Closure Risk)
- Thawing food at room temperature >2 hours
- Operating fryer without certified personnel
- Undercooking ground meat (below 155°F)
- No food handler certificates on file

### 6.2 NYC Health Inspection Points
- **Grade A:** 0-13 points
- **Grade B:** 14-27 points  
- **Grade C:** 28+ points (may face closure)

### 6.3 Penalties
- **First critical violation:** $200-$2,000 fine
- **Repeat violations:** Possible permit suspension
- **Serious violations:** Immediate closure order

---

## 7. Daily Compliance Checklist

### Morning Opening:
- [ ] Verify all food handlers have valid certificates
- [ ] Check fryer certifications posted
- [ ] Verify refrigerator temperature ≤ 41°F
- [ ] Check freezer temperature ≤ 0°F
- [ ] Inspect food storage order in refrigerators
- [ ] Ensure sanitizer buckets at proper concentration

### During Service:
- [ ] Record cooking temperatures (minimum every 2 hours)
- [ ] Verify certified cook at each active fryer
- [ ] Monitor thawing foods (maximum 2 hours if using cold water)
- [ ] Check hand washing compliance

### Closing:
- [ ] Clean and sanitize all surfaces
- [ ] Properly store all foods
- [ ] Empty and clean sanitizer buckets
- [ ] Document any compliance issues

---

## 8. Staffing for Compliance

### 8.1 Minimum Staffing Levels
Based on NYC regulations and operational needs:

**Lunch Service (11 AM - 2 PM):**
- 2 certified cooks (if using 2 fryers)
- 1 prep cook
- 1-2 front-of-house staff

**Dinner Service (5 PM - 9 PM):**
- 2-3 certified cooks (if using 2-3 fryers)
- 1 prep cook
- 2-3 front-of-house staff

**Why Additional Cook May Be Required:**
- If forecast shows >50 orders/hour AND using 2+ fryers
- Each fryer MUST have dedicated certified operator during peak
- Prevents safety violations and maintains quality
